Introduction

The Transaction SOP outlines the procedures for the Transaction Team in managing user transactions, monitoring for potential fraud, and resolving deposit and withdrawal inquiries.

Roles and Responsibilities

  • Monitor for suspicious transaction activities, including high-value deposits and coin mixing behaviors.
  • Resolve inquiries related to deposits and withdrawals from users and customer service (CS) agents.
  • Escalate complex cases to the Risk/Fraud/KYC team as needed for further review.

Step-by-Step Procedure

1. Initial Transaction Review

Begin by reviewing all user transactions, particularly for signs of suspicious behavior. Flag high-value deposits from unverified accounts and any deposit-withdrawal sequences that resemble coin mixing.

2. Resolving Deposit and Withdrawal Issues

When CS agents escalate inquiries regarding deposit or withdrawal issues, follow these steps:

  • Incorrect Network/Address: Inform the user that funds are unrecoverable if they used the wrong network or address.
  • Pending Transactions: Advise the user to wait for additional blockchain confirmations.

3. Suspicious Activity Protocol

If you detect patterns of suspicious behavior, such as rapid withdrawals with no wagering, escalate the case to the Risk and Fraud teams for further analysis.

Escalation Protocols

For complex or unresolved cases, escalate the issue to the appropriate teams via Slack. Clearly document the reason for escalation, include details on suspected behavior, and tag relevant team members.
